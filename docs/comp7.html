Models

The 2 models i will be comparing are listed below

  • AGILE
  • Waterfall

the AGILE model is a development cycle that is designed to be integrated into large multi-disciplinary teams. Software development is broken into short goal driven sprints. A sprint can last from 1-4 weeks, and contains planing, development, and testing. The model promotes the use of constant communication. Meetings called 'SCRUMS' are a space for talking with stakeholders.

üëç Advantages of AGILE

  • Highly flexible and can adapt to suit the needs of the team, as it's iterative and incremental.
  • Users and testing are involved throughout development so issues are quickly identified.

üëé Disadvantages of AGILE

  • Difficult to manage as product creep may occur.
  • Not always suitable for smaller teams or individuals working alone.

The Waterfall model is a linear approach to software development, a "think first code second", where requirements gathering and planning are completed beforehand. Each stage results in a fully fulfilled product, realizing the established goals and targets before the next cycle can begin.

üëé Disadvantages of Waterfall

  • Difficulty adapting to changing requirements and arising issues
  • Stakeholders are not involved until the near end.

üëçAdvantages of Waterfall

  • Simply structured around achieving the final resulting product
  • Perfect model for smaller teams and projects
